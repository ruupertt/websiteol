03 // ROBOT

AUTONOMY,
FROM THE PCB UP.

Two robots share a common architecture built around 360-degree vision, four-wheel omnidirectional movement, custom sensing and a C++ control stack.

EXPLODED_ASSEMBLY // ITERATION_02

SYSTEM_01

360° Vision

A custom hyperbolic mirror gives the OpenMV camera a panoramic view of the field. The vision pipeline identifies the orange ball, goals and field geometry for real-time positioning and decision-making.

  • OpenMV camera
  • Custom mirror profile
  • Real-time object detection

A_01

Orbit logic

The attacking robot calculates an angle offset from the ball and varies it with distance, allowing it to move around the ball rather than simply driving into it.

angle = orbit(ball_angle, distance); drive.vector(angle, speed);

A_02

Line avoidance

Forty-eight phototransistors form inner and outer sensing rings. Sensor clusters estimate the white boundary line angle so the robot can correct before leaving the field.

clusters = scan(line_ring); escape(midpoint(clusters));

A_03

Team coordination

HC-05 Bluetooth modules support communication between the two robots, enabling role awareness and coordinated attacker-defender behaviour.

peer.send(robot_state); role = resolve(team_state);
